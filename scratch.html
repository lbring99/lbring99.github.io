Why I Mountain Bike

There have been many people that ask me why I mountain bike. Not only do I love the adrenaline of flying down a mountain or the feeling of accomplishment of climbing a mountain and looking down at where you had come from, but there is something special that happens when you go through the physical pain. When it's just you, your bike, and the beautiful world. Your mind hits a focus and a clarity that is unlike anything I have ever experienced. I like to tell people that mountain biking is 30% physical and 70% mental capabilities. It is pure determination that gets you to the top and helps you keep going. Our bodies can do amazing things if we can push aside the physical fatigue.


Every time I have been in a mountain bike race the initial pain and realization of what I'm about to put myself through hits. I think to myself "This sucks! Why would I ever do this? I just started and I still have 13ish miles left." Yet I did several races, why? You hit a point in the race where your heart rate slows and your body adjusts to the constant physical exertion. You begin to look around and appreciate the beautiful trees, sage and the short down hill that had a rock garden. And when you finish and realize what you have just accomplished it's unlike anything else, so I kept doing it.

Essential Gear

What you need before you hit the trails:

  1. The Bike
    • Full suspension for technical trails
    • Set up tubeless — flat tires end rides
    • 29" wheels for speed and rollover
  2. Safety Gear
    • Helmet — full-face or trail depending on terrain
    • Knee pads — you will thank yourself
    • Gloves for grip and protection
  3. Pack Essentials
    • At least 2 liters of water
    • Spare tube, multi-tool, CO2
    • Snacks — you bonk faster than you think
